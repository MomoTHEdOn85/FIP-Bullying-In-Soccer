Bullying Introduction

What is bullying in soccer?

Based on Bullying Canada, 47% of Canadian parents have a child who was being bullied. Especially, when there is a battle between two teams against each other, the vibe could be intense in soccer. Bullying even happens within a team, and also might be received from different parties, such as coaches, players’ parents, families and friends, and audiences, etc.


There are three forms of bullying:



Verbal bullying

Nasty/ cruel nicknames calling, taunting, rudeness, threats of violence and harm to a teammate.



Physical bullying

Any form of physical violence toward the victims.



Relational bullying

Social isolating/ excluding the victims, gossiping, hurtful talk and embarrassment of the victims in front of others.

The Problem

Bullying in soccer is a problem at all levels, from youth to professional. It often happens in the culture of the sport when one team is against another team. The culture of soccer puts players into a situation where they have no choice but to be aggressive. It is also the physical nature of the game when players are running and tackling each other, there are opportunities for anyone to get hurt or insulted by other players.


There are various situations that victims get hurt physically or even mentally. The bullying experience does cause trauma which stays in their sports experience when the victims especially at a young age, do not feel supported and appreciated. That leads to more mental health issues while the problem does not being solved.

Empathy

Empathy

It is a great idea to understand others by putting ourselves in their shoes. When unfortunate events happen, how would you like others to treat you if you were the victim? Would you want everyone picks on you, and be aggressive toward you? What if the victim is your loved one, your family member, your brother, sister, or best friend? The list goes on, you will not want to see anyone hurts your loved one, it is painful.






Helping or Getting Help

How can you help? or stop the bullying?

Get help from professionals or talk to the people you trust for reaching out about the issue.Find resources that might be helpful for you.


Stop Bullying

Do not be someone’s darkness, be supportive, and respectful. Everyone deserves a chance to play soccer, regardless of their abilities. We do not want any aggressive or harmful behaviour in our games!
